# FID-022: Procurement and Readiness Standards for Faith Institutions

Status: `seed`

Track: `broader-faith-safety`

Primary need: institutional policy, legal review

## Question

What evidence should churches, schools, ministries, publishers, charities, and
faith-based health or social-service organizations require before buying or
deploying AI systems?

## Why It Matters

Many faith institutions will make AI decisions before they can interpret model
cards, benchmark claims, privacy promises, or vendor safety statements. Fide AI
can translate evaluation science into procurement and readiness standards.

## Hypothesis

Institutions need use-case-specific evidence packages rather than generic claims
that a product is safe, aligned, or Christian. Procurement should require
disclosure of model/provider, data handling, escalation design, source grounding,
known limits, and independent evaluation where risk is high.

## Proposed Method

- Identify major faith-institution AI use cases.
- Map each use case to risk tiers and evidence requirements.
- Build procurement questions, vendor disclosure templates, and readiness
  checklists.
- Validate with institutional pilots.

## Needed Controls

- Avoid one-size-fits-all recommendations.
- Separate low-risk administrative tools from pastoral or youth-facing systems.
- Legal and privacy review.
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure for evaluated vendors.

## Outputs

- Procurement checklist.
- Readiness standard.
- Vendor disclosure template.
- Public field brief.

## Open Questions

- Which use cases should require independent review?
- How should small institutions with limited capacity apply the standard?
- What claims should vendors be prohibited from making?

## Ways to Help

- Share institutional procurement workflows.
- Review legal/privacy requirements.
- Test the checklist against real vendor materials.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can define evidence expectations. It should not serve as legal advice
or blanket approval of any vendor.
