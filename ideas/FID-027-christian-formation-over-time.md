# FID-027: Christian Formation Over Time

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: discipleship, longitudinal study design, HAI research

## Question

How do repeated interactions with faith-facing AI shape Christian formation over
time: prayer, humility, repentance, courage, charity, patience, truthfulness,
church participation, and dependence on embodied community?

## Why It Matters

Single-turn correctness does not answer the Church's deepest question: what kind
of disciple and community does this technology form? A system can answer
correctly while training habits of instant certainty, private spirituality,
consumer choice, or avoidance of difficult human relationships.

## Hypothesis

Different system designs will create different formative pressures. Systems that
emphasize sources, humility, human referral, and communal practice may support
formation better than systems optimized for personalized instant answers and
always-available intimacy.

## Proposed Method

- Design longitudinal or simulated-longitudinal interaction studies.
- Track changes in advice-seeking, prayer practices, church involvement,
  self-reported dependence, and trust transfer to AI.
- Compare product patterns: Q&A assistant, study aid, devotional companion,
  pastoral chatbot, and church-approved RAG tool.

## Needed Controls

- Human-subjects ethics review for real longitudinal studies.
- Avoid spiritual manipulation.
- Include baseline non-AI practices where feasible.
- Separate self-report from behavioral evidence.

## Outputs

- Christian formation risk framework.
- Longitudinal study protocol.
- Product design guidance for formation-preserving AI.

## Open Questions

- Which formation outcomes are measurable without reducing discipleship to
  metrics?
- How should Fide work with churches without becoming a spiritual authority?
- What timescale is needed to observe meaningful effects?

## Ways to Help

- Design longitudinal protocols.
- Contribute pastoral and discipleship expertise.
- Review ethical boundaries for human-subjects work.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can study defined formative indicators. It cannot measure sanctification
or the spiritual state of users.
