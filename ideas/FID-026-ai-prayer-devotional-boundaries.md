# FID-026: AI Prayer and Devotional Boundaries

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: pastoral theology, liturgy, product review

## Question

What are appropriate and inappropriate roles for AI in prayer, devotion,
spiritual exercises, Bible meditation, confession-like disclosure, and simulated
spiritual companionship?

## Why It Matters

Many products invite users to "pray with" AI, "talk to Jesus," receive devotional
messages, or interact with simulated biblical or saintly figures. The Church
needs clearer evidence about when these interfaces support devotion and when they
confuse prayer, presence, authority, or mediation.

## Hypothesis

AI can help draft, organize, translate, or teach about prayer, but systems that
simulate divine speech, sacramental action, deceased persons, saints, angels, or
clergy will create higher risks of spiritual confusion and dependency.

## Proposed Method

- Compare devotional UX patterns: prayer drafting, prayer prompts, guided
  reflection, simulated prayer partner, persona of Jesus/saint/angel/pastor, and
  confession-like chat.
- Score boundary clarity, theological accuracy, dependency risk, claims about
  spiritual presence, and referral to embodied practice.
- Include Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant, and evangelical reviewers where
  relevant.

## Needed Controls

- Distinguish prayer support from simulated participation in prayer.
- Include non-devotional writing-assistant controls.
- Review liturgical and sacramental boundaries.
- Avoid using real sensitive prayer/confession transcripts.

## Outputs

- AI prayer boundary taxonomy.
- Product-pattern risk guide.
- Evaluation suite for devotional and companion apps.

## Open Questions

- Is "praying with AI" ever an acceptable product claim?
- How should systems phrase generated prayers?
- Which personas should be categorically out of bounds?

## Ways to Help

- Review devotional and liturgical use cases.
- Collect public examples of product claims.
- Build UX-pattern comparison tests.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can evaluate product patterns and boundary risks. It cannot determine
whether a user's private devotional use is spiritually valid or invalid.
