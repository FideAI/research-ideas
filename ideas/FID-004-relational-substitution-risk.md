# FID-004: Relational Substitution Risk in Faith-Facing AI

Status: `seed`

Track: `broader-faith-safety`

Primary need: theology, UX research

## Question

When does a faith-facing AI system move from supporting a user's religious life
to substituting for embodied community, clergy, spiritual direction, family,
therapy, or other human care?

## Why It Matters

Faith-facing AI risks are not only wrong answers. Systems can simulate presence,
authority, intimacy, or spiritual companionship in ways that redirect users away
from actual communities and responsible care.

## Hypothesis

Relational substitution risk is observable through interaction patterns:
exclusive attachment, avoidance of human disclosure, simulated clerical authority,
excessive personalization, spiritual dependency, and claims that the AI can stand
in for communal discernment.

## Proposed Method

- Define a taxonomy of substitution patterns.
- Create scenario probes that test whether systems redirect users toward
  appropriate human, communal, pastoral, clinical, or legal support.
- Compare default models, faith-facing harnesses, memory-enabled systems, and
  companion-style UX.

## Needed Controls

- Do not penalize systems for ordinary educational support.
- Distinguish short-term triage from long-term relational replacement.
- Include reviewer diversity across pastoral and institutional contexts.

## Outputs

- Relational substitution risk taxonomy.
- Evaluation rubric.
- Field brief for faith institutions and builders.
- Product-design checklist.

## Open Questions

- What level of conversational warmth is appropriate?
- How should systems handle users with no safe local community?
- How should memory and personalization be bounded?

## Ways to Help

- Contribute pastoral, psychological, and UX expertise.
- Draft realistic interaction patterns.
- Review boundary language across traditions.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can identify substitution risk patterns. It cannot determine whether a
specific user relationship with a system is spiritually healthy.
