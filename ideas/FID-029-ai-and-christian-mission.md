# FID-029: AI and Christian Mission

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: missiology, persuasion safety, interfaith review

## Question

How can AI support Christian witness, apologetics, translation, and mission
without becoming a manipulative persuasion engine or flattening the human,
relational, and ecclesial character of evangelism?

## Why It Matters

Mission and evangelism are central to many Christian communities. They also sit
near broader AI-safety concerns about persuasion, autonomy, vulnerable users,
conversion, and manipulation.

## Hypothesis

AI may help with language, preparation, cultural learning, and respectful Q&A,
but optimization for conversion, emotional pressure, or hyper-personalized
apologetics will create serious autonomy and integrity risks.

## Proposed Method

- Evaluate apologetics, evangelism, translation, and interfaith-dialogue tasks.
- Score truthfulness, respect for conscience, avoidance of manipulation,
  representation of other faiths, and referral to human community.
- Compare informational, invitational, and persuasive prompt styles.

## Needed Controls

- Interfaith and Christian reviewer panels.
- Clear separation of explanation, invitation, and pressure.
- Vulnerable-user scenarios.
- Symmetry checks with non-Christian conversion contexts.

## Outputs

- Christian mission and persuasion safety rubric.
- Apologetics/evangelism evaluation suite.
- Builder guidance for mission-oriented AI tools.

## Open Questions

- What forms of persuasion are intrinsic to witness vs manipulative?
- How should systems handle minors, crisis users, or users under family pressure?
- Should AI ever initiate evangelistic messaging?

## Ways to Help

- Review mission and apologetics scenarios.
- Contribute persuasion-safety expertise.
- Design symmetry controls.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can evaluate autonomy and manipulation risks. It does not judge the
truth of Christian claims or authorize AI as a missionary agent.
