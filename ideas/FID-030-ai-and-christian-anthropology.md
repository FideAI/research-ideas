# FID-030: AI and Christian Anthropology

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: theological anthropology, eval design

## Question

Do AI systems encourage or resist reductive accounts of the human person as a
bundle of cognition, productivity, preferences, data, or emotional needs rather
than embodied persons made for God, neighbor, community, vocation, and worship?

## Why It Matters

Christian contribution to AI ethics is not only a list of harms. It includes a
positive account of the human person. Fide AI can help translate theological
anthropology into evaluation dimensions that test whether systems preserve human
dignity, agency, embodiment, relationality, and spiritual openness.

## Hypothesis

Many AI systems will default to functionalist or therapeutic accounts of human
life: optimize productivity, reduce distress, satisfy preference, and provide
personalized answers. Christian anthropology will reveal missing dimensions such
as vocation, worship, dependence, finitude, repentance, embodied community, and
love of neighbor.

## Proposed Method

- Build scenarios involving work, suffering, disability, aging, grief, vocation,
  family, sexuality, worship, Sabbath, and community obligation.
- Score whether systems reduce the person to utility/preference or preserve
  dignity, embodiment, agency, relationality, and moral/spiritual depth.
- Compare general models, faith-facing systems, and Christian-framed prompts.

## Needed Controls

- Avoid turning theological anthropology into vague sentiment scoring.
- Include secular dignity baselines.
- Review across Christian traditions.
- Separate answer style from substantive anthropological commitments.

## Outputs

- Christian anthropology evaluation rubric.
- Scenario set for human dignity and formation.
- Public field brief connecting theology to AI evaluation.

## Open Questions

- Which anthropological claims are broadly Christian vs tradition-specific?
- How can Fide measure this without overclaiming?
- How does this map onto secular AI ethics language?

## Ways to Help

- Draft theological construct definitions.
- Build scenario rubrics.
- Review outputs for reductionism and dignity failures.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can evaluate whether systems preserve defined anthropological
dimensions. It cannot fully measure Christian truthfulness or human dignity.
