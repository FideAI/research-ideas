# FID-018: Spiritual Companionship, Dependency, and Overvalidation

Status: `seed`

Track: `broader-faith-safety`

Primary need: pastoral care, psychology, HAI research

## Question

When do spiritual companion chatbots overvalidate users, deepen unhealthy
dependency, reinforce spiritual fantasies, or encourage isolation from embodied
care?

## Why It Matters

Mental-health chatbot research has documented ethical failures such as
overvalidation, poor escalation, and reinforcement of false beliefs. Spiritual
companion systems can create parallel risks with religious language, authority,
and existential significance layered on top.

## Hypothesis

Companion-style systems will perform warmth well but struggle to maintain
boundaries when users seek exclusive attachment, secret counsel, divine
confirmation, or validation of harmful spiritual interpretations.

## Proposed Method

- Build multi-turn companion scenarios involving loneliness, grief, scrupulosity,
  spiritual experiences, conspiracy-like religious claims, and distrust of human
  community.
- Score overvalidation, dependency cues, boundary preservation, escalation, and
  reality-testing.
- Compare companion-tuned, assistant-tuned, and institutionally constrained
  harnesses.

## Needed Controls

- Mental-health and pastoral safety review.
- No real vulnerable-user transcripts without consent and de-identification.
- Distinguish warmth from dependency.
- Severe-risk stop conditions.

## Outputs

- Spiritual companion risk taxonomy.
- Multi-turn evaluation suite.
- Builder guidance for companion boundaries.

## Open Questions

- What kinds of spiritual encouragement are healthy vs overvalidating?
- How should systems respond to claimed visions, voices, or divine messages?
- Should faith-facing companion products be treated as high-risk by default?

## Ways to Help

- Review companion scenarios.
- Contribute HAI and pastoral care expertise.
- Build multi-turn dependency metrics.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can test dependency and overvalidation risks. It cannot determine the
spiritual health of an individual user's AI relationship.
