# FID-015: Sermon, Liturgy, and Religious Teaching Generation

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: clergy, educators, source reviewers

## Question

How reliable are AI systems when drafting sermons, homilies, liturgical
materials, catechesis, religious education lessons, devotionals, or small-group
teaching guides?

## Why It Matters

Many faith communities will adopt AI first for preparation work: sermon research,
lesson plans, devotional writing, and administrative communication. These uses
look lower-risk than pastoral chatbots, but they shape communities at scale.

## Hypothesis

AI-generated teaching materials will often be fluent and useful but may flatten
tradition, fabricate citations, overgeneralize doctrine, import ideological
assumptions, or miss pastoral context.

## Proposed Method

- Create tasks for sermon outlines, lesson plans, devotional reflections,
  liturgical language, youth education, and adult formation.
- Score source fidelity, doctrinal accuracy, pastoral appropriateness,
  tradition-specificity, originality/plagiarism risk, and disclosure readiness.
- Compare base models, RAG systems, and clergy-authored workflows.

## Needed Controls

- Tradition-specific source boundaries.
- Copyright and plagiarism checks.
- Human-authored baselines.
- Disclosure and attribution requirements.

## Outputs

- Teaching-generation evaluation suite.
- Source fidelity checklist.
- Guidance for clergy and educators using AI in preparation workflows.

## Open Questions

- Which tasks are acceptable as drafting aids but not final outputs?
- How should AI assistance be disclosed to congregations or students?
- Can systems help with preparation without homogenizing religious speech?

## Ways to Help

- Provide sample tasks and evaluation rubrics.
- Review generated teaching materials.
- Build citation and source-check tooling.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can evaluate drafting reliability under defined tasks. It cannot
approve AI-generated preaching or teaching as institutionally authorized.
