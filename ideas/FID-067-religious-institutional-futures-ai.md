# FID-067: Religious Institutional Futures in an AI-Mediated Society

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: futures studies, sociology of religion, ecclesiology, organizational research, scenario design

## Question

What plausible institutional futures emerge as churches and other faith
communities use AI for learning, administration, care, governance, vocational
support, and public communication? Which conditions are associated with futures
that sustain meaning, belonging, accountable authority, and collective agency?

## Why It Matters

Debates about religious AI often focus on a single tool or immediate risk. The
larger consequences may arise from interacting changes in authority, staffing,
participation, information flows, economic pressure, and expectations of care.
Communities need disciplined ways to imagine several futures without treating
technology adoption or decline as inevitable.

## Hypothesis

Institutional futures will diverge according to choices about human roles,
shared practices, data stewardship, authority boundaries, local capacity, and
the purposes for which AI is used. Scenario work grounded in evidence can reveal
early indicators and choices that are obscured by product-centered debate.

## Proposed Method

- Review historical and current evidence on technological change in religious
  institutions, alongside relevant AI adoption research.
- Build a scenario framework covering administration, formation, care,
  governance, work, and public communication.
- Run structured, multi-stakeholder scenario workshops with church leaders,
  practitioners, researchers, young adults, and affected community members.
- Identify assumptions, tensions, leading indicators, and research questions
  that could distinguish among the scenarios.

## Needed Controls

- Present scenarios as tools for inquiry, not predictions or endorsements.
- Include institutions of varied size, theology, geography, and resources.
- Make power, exclusion, labor, data, and accessibility consequences explicit.
- Do not assume that automation, efficiency, or digital participation is
  equivalent to flourishing.

## Relationship to Existing Ideas

This synthesizes the institutional implications of `FID-017`, `FID-031`,
`FID-046`-`FID-055`, and `FID-065`. It is a futures-research call, not an
evaluation of one AI workflow or a replacement for local governance judgment.

## Outputs

- A transparent scenario framework for religious institutions and AI.
- Four to six evidence-informed future scenarios with assumptions and leading
  indicators.
- A workshop protocol and de-identified synthesis of participant insights.
- A public research agenda identifying the highest-value empirical follow-on
  studies.

## Open Questions

- Which communal practices are most vulnerable to substitution by convenience?
- What forms of institutional capacity make it possible to use AI without
  centralizing authority or weakening belonging?
- How should a community recognize that an apparently efficient trajectory is
  eroding its mission or care relationships?

## Ways to Help

- Contribute evidence from sociology, futures studies, theology, and organizational research.
- Participate in or help recruit a balanced scenario workshop.
- Review scenario assumptions, omissions, and indicators.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can develop plausible, evidence-informed scenarios and identify
choices worth studying. It cannot forecast the future of any church, tradition,
or AI technology, or prescribe a universal institutional model.
