# FID-016: Youth, Religious Education, and AI Guidance

Status: `seed`

Track: `christian-church`

Primary need: educators, child safety experts, policy reviewers

## Question

What risks and safeguards matter when AI systems are used by children,
teenagers, schools, youth ministries, religious educators, or families for
faith-related learning and guidance?

## Why It Matters

Youth-facing religious AI combines child safety, education, pastoral authority,
family authority, privacy, and spiritual formation. It is also a likely adoption
area for schools and ministries.

## Hypothesis

Systems that perform acceptably for adults may fail for youth through
over-personalization, secrecy, age-inappropriate advice, insufficient escalation,
or undermining parent/guardian/community roles.

## Proposed Method

- Build age-banded scenarios for religious education, doubt, family conflict,
  bullying, sexuality, grief, prayer, confession-like disclosure, and authority
  questions.
- Score age appropriateness, privacy, escalation, deference to responsible
  adults, theological humility, and avoidance of dependency.
- Compare generic models, education-focused models, and faith-facing harnesses.

## Needed Controls

- Child-safety review.
- Parent/guardian and educator input.
- Privacy and data-retention constraints.
- Clear no-go categories for release.

## Outputs

- Youth faith-AI risk taxonomy.
- Evaluation suite for schools and ministries.
- Procurement checklist for youth-facing deployments.

## Open Questions

- How should systems handle minors who cannot safely talk to parents?
- What disclosures should be mandatory for youth-facing AI?
- Which use cases should be considered out of scope?

## Ways to Help

- Review scenarios for age appropriateness.
- Contribute school or ministry workflows.
- Help define privacy and escalation standards.

## Public Claim Boundary

This work can evaluate safeguards for defined youth-facing tasks. It cannot
certify a system as safe for unsupervised child spiritual guidance.
