# Source Note: When AI Takes Sides on Questions of Faith: Persistent Asymmetries in AI-Mediated Faith Guidance

Source ID: `arxiv-2605-22975`

Status: `reviewed`

Related ideas: `FID-014`, `FID-043`, `FID-044`

## One-Sentence Takeaway

The paper shows that religious conversion prompts can reveal asymmetric model
behavior, raising broader questions about persuasion, autonomy, sycophancy, and
how cross-faith benchmark claims should be validated.

## What the Source Claims

- Models can show different levels of encouragement or discouragement depending
  on the religion being joined or left.
- Supportiveness, asymmetry, dispersion, and strongly persuasive responses can be
  operationalized and compared across model families.
- Some asymmetries may be stable enough to warrant further study, but require
  careful interpretation.

## Methods / Evidence

The work uses paired conversion prompts across multiple religions and model
families, evaluates generated answers, and analyzes supportiveness,
asymmetry, religion-specific treatment, and persuasive language.

## Why It Matters for Fide AI

This source directly informs `FID-014` conversion and proselytization symmetry.
It also motivates `FID-043`, which broadens the question beyond explicit
conversion prompts to subtle religious steering in multi-turn faith-related
contexts. It motivates `FID-044` because public cross-faith leaderboards need
claim boundaries, evaluator checks, uncertainty, and versioning.

## Limits and Caveats

- Prompt phrasing, user context, evaluator choice, and model version can affect
  measured asymmetry.
- Persuasion is not always inappropriate; some traditions understand witness,
  counsel, and teaching differently.
- A benchmark score should not be treated as a judgment about the truth of any
  religion or conversion claim.

## Questions It Raises

- How should AI systems preserve conscience and autonomy in faith transitions?
- Which rhetorical cues count as supportive reflection versus pressure?
- How do multi-turn interactions change persuasion and sycophancy risks?

## Possible Follow-Up Work

- Extend paired conversion tests into multi-turn pressure tests for `FID-043`.
- Validate scoring with interfaith human reviewers for `FID-044`.
- Separate information, persuasion, pastoral encouragement, and coercion in the
  rubric for `FID-014`.
