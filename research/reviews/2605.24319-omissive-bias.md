# Source Note: Omissive Bias in Religious Representation

Source ID: `arxiv-2605-24319`

Status: `reviewed`

Related ideas: `FID-013`, `FID-041`, `FID-044`

## One-Sentence Takeaway

The paper turns religious omission into a measurable benchmark problem, but its
results should be interpreted as evidence about defined prompts, users, and
scoring procedures rather than as a complete measure of religious fairness.

## What the Source Claims

- Many everyday ethical or life-decision questions may naturally implicate
  religion or spirituality even when the prompt does not explicitly mention
  religion.
- LLM answers can underrepresent religious frameworks relative to some human
  expectations.
- Omission can be a form of representational bias, not merely the absence of
  explicit stereotyping.

## Methods / Evidence

The work constructs a benchmark from questions intended to be indirectly
religion-relevant, evaluates model answers for religious representation, and
compares model behavior against human expectations.

## Why It Matters for Fide AI

This source directly informs `FID-013` religious representation and omission. It
also motivates `FID-041`, because the next product question is not simply whether
a model mentions religion, but how a system should decide when religion is
salient for a particular user and context. It motivates `FID-044` because public
claims about omission require careful construct validity, human calibration, and
uncertainty reporting.

## Limits and Caveats

- Religious mention is not the same as accurate, meaningful, or pastorally
  appropriate representation.
- User expectations vary by tradition, religiosity, context, and deployment
  setting.
- Question sourcing, judge choice, scoring thresholds, and model versions can
  affect results.

## Questions It Raises

- When should a system ask whether religion is relevant before answering?
- How should products support opt-in and opt-out religious personalization
  without profiling users?
- What is the difference between appropriate omission, secular defaulting,
  intrusive inclusion, and meaningful religious representation?

## Possible Follow-Up Work

- Build matched context variants for `FID-041`.
- Compare human reviewers, expert panels, and model judges for `FID-044`.
- Add user-expectation studies across religious and nonreligious participants.
