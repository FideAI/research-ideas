# Funding-Inspired Research Call Map

This note records how selected external funding priorities informed new Fide AI
research questions. It is an agenda map, not a representation that a funder has
endorsed Fide AI, its claims, or an individual call. Each call remains a public
research invitation subject to the repository's claim boundaries.

## Design Principle

Funder priorities are useful when they expose an important, underdeveloped
question that fits Fide AI's mission. They are not a reason to reshape a call
around a grant category that does not genuinely fit. The calls below are framed
as durable research questions first; potential funding lanes are secondary.

## Religion, Science, and Society

John Templeton Foundation's [Religion, Science, and Society](https://www.templeton.org/funding-areas/religion-science-and-society)
area asks research questions on intelligence, meaning making, and imagined
futures. Its Intelligence topic is a strong fit for work that makes an explicit
concept of intelligence and relates artificial systems to wisdom, personhood,
or collective life. Its Imagined Futures topic supports disciplined inquiry into
how communities understand and navigate technological change.

| Fide call | External fit | Why it fits |
|---|---|---|
| `FID-064` | RSS: Intelligence; secondary Imagined Futures | Tests how AI changes collective intelligence, dissent, and accountable communal judgment. |
| `FID-067` | RSS: Imagined Futures | Develops evidence-informed scenarios for religious institutions in an AI-mediated society. |
| `FID-068` | RSS: Meaning Making, secondary | Studies practices through which young people interpret AI use in relation to agency, relationship, and purpose. |

## Individual Freedom and Free Markets

John Templeton Foundation's [Individual Freedom and Free Markets](https://www.templeton.org/funding-areas/individual-freedom-free-markets)
area highlights political economy, dispersed knowledge, voluntary association,
freedom of conscience, decentralized authority, and how advanced technologies
may centralize or distribute power. Research in this lane should investigate
those claims rather than advocate a particular political or regulatory result.

| Fide call | External fit | Why it fits |
|---|---|---|
| `FID-065` | IFFM: Research at Vanguard | Examines AI's effect on local knowledge, institutional learning, and faith-based civil society's problem-solving capacity. |
| `FID-066` | IFFM: Research at Vanguard; Freedom Catalysts context | Examines platform-gateway conditions for conscience, plural association, explanation, appeal, portability, and exit. |
| `FID-064` | IFFM: secondary | Studies the decision-level effects of concentrated or distributed informational authority. |

## Mathematical and Physical Sciences

The current [Mathematical and Physical Sciences](https://www.templeton.org/funding-areas/mathematical-physical-sciences)
cycle is focused on research beyond the Standard Model and extraterrestrial
technosignatures that uses AI or machine learning substantively. Fide AI's
current calls are **not** a direct fit for that grant category.

The area nevertheless provides a methodological reminder: make model
assumptions explicit, separate simulation from observation, and publish
reproducible methods. `FID-064` draws on that rigor through transparent network
and agent-based models, but should not be positioned as an MPS funding proposal.

## Other Faith-Aligned Foundation Inspiration

The [Lilly Endowment Faith and AI Initiative](https://lillyendowment.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/faith-and-ai-initiative-proposal-guidelines-2026-updated-may.pdf)
has emphasized helping youth and young adults draw on Christian wisdom and
practices as they navigate AI and digital technology. That informs `FID-068`'s
research focus on practice design, measures, and safeguarding; it does not mean
the call reproduces Lilly's program or eligibility requirements.

The [Louisville Institute Grant for Researchers](https://louisville-institute.org/programs-grants-and-fellowships/grants/grant-for-researchers/)
supports research that connects Christian life, institutions, and ministry with
scholarly inquiry. Its emphasis on research that serves the North American
church makes `FID-065`, `FID-067`, and `FID-068` especially natural questions
for academic-practitioner teams.

## Relationship to Fide AI's Current Application

Fide AI's current Religion, Science, and Society concept investigates Christian
conceptions of intelligence, wisdom, testimony, authority, and collective
discernment in relation to AI. `FID-064` is the clearest public research-call
extension of that concept: it opens a methodologically concrete question about
how AI-mediated systems affect collective inquiry without reducing the broader
proposal to one authority or governance issue.

`FID-067` supplies the complementary future-facing inquiry. `FID-065`,
`FID-066`, and `FID-068` expand the portfolio into local institutional capacity,
plural association, and formation-oriented research where the questions are
distinct and fundable on their own merits.
